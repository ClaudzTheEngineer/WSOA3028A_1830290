As we may retroactively think.

by Claudio Surmon

“As we may think”, was an essay written in 1945 by Vannevar Bush in which he speculated about the aspects of life in an information society. One of the most influential visions he had was of the memex and its use in storing information for ease of access, among some other ideas.

Vannevar Bush

Vannevar Bush was an American engineer who headed the office of the U.S. Scientific Research and Development which carried out the research for the Manhattan project among other projects.

The MEMEX

Not to be confused with a meme for people from generation ‘X’, the “memex” was a thought of to be a device into which a person could compress and store all their books, records and other information. This information would be stored on microtape and then subsequently projected, so that the storage would remain small and compact. Users could record associative links between articles which could then be shared. This idea of the associative link is credited as the inspiration for the hypertext system created in the 1960’s.

Today’s MEMEX

The system’s memory is very reminiscent of resources like Wikipedia, in which hyperlinks allow readers to find associated articles or do deep dives on certain topics.

The window on the top of the system that would allow for the user to input longhand notes, photos or articles into the photographic memory is similar to flatbed scanners.

All in all, the inventions thought of by Vannevar in this essay are wonderfully archaic in their design, but beautifully futuristic in their function, it allows us to wonder what technology will be like in another 50 years.

References

V. Bush, “As we may think,” The Atlantic, pp. 1-10, 1945.
