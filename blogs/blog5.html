Meta Meta-data blog post

by Claudio Surmon

The Latin origin word, meta, is used to describe something that is self-referential, and data is a discrete piece of information. Combine these definitions and you get self-referential information, or information about information.

In a computing context, meta-tags and meta-data is the information that a webpage stores about the information stored within the page. These can include, but are in no way limited to, the character set used by the page, instructions for bots, the owner and various other information the user will not normally see.

Are they anywhere else?

Meta-tags are used elsewhere in the computing world, not just for websites. A trivial example being digital photos, contained within the data are meta-tags that store the aperture, focal length, what white balance was used, what ISO, exposure time, location data and more.

Null Island

A funny occurrence that happens due to poorly designed software, is the seemly existent “null island” at 0°N, 0°E in the Gulf of Guinea. Large amounts of photos seem to have been taken at this place as some software would enter (0,0) into the geolocation metatag erroneously
